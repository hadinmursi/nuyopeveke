Jay Leno Admitted To The Rumor That He Never Touched Any Of His Tonight Show Pays

Jay Leno is worth a fortune today, despite the fact that he even took a pay cut to keep The Tonight Show on the air. Nowadays, Leno has a worth of $450 million and chances are, he was able to retain a lot of that money. Fans have praised Leno for being humble with the way he lives and, in addition, he confirmed a crazy urban legend speaking alongside Adam Carolla. Leno confirmed that he never touched any of his Tonight Show money.

Carolla asks, "The legend is, is that you always kept all those Tonight Show checks. And made your money doing live performances."

Leno did not hesitate when revealing that was completely true.

Leno says, "You got to be hungry. I used to live week to week. I lived on the money I made as a standup. I wanted to be a comedian. I never thought TV was a job that would last you."

Leno went on to confirm that he never touched any of his Tonight Show salary "until the very end."

Carolla was stunned by the response, especially given Leno's habits of spending on cars, and giving back to his Tonight Show employees back in the day.

Leno was able to not only survive, but confirmed that the money is still there in an account.

Jay Leno Was Wise With His Profits From The Very Start Of His Comedy Career

Jay Leno went from minimum wage at McDonald's to making millions. CNBC took a closer look at his rise. One thing that was evident is, from the start, Leno was wise when it came to saving his money.

“Then I got to the point where the comedy money was, like, five times the other money, so I decided to flip it around and save the comedy money,” Leno says. “I would always spend the lesser amount of what the two were.”

Even when he got on The Tonight Show, that mentality stayed the same in order to motivate himself.

“I pretended as if I didn’t even have the ‘Tonight Show’ job. You know, when you start making money, you get lazy. I wanted to make sure I always had that hunger, so I never looked. It would go directly into a bank.”

A great story, and one that is very different compared to other tragic tales in Hollywood of the opposite holding true.
