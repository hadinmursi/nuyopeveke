Early Life and Military Service

J. R. Martinez, born on June 14, 1983, in Shreveport, Louisiana, had an unconventional upbringing. While he was born in the United States, Martinez spent his early years in El Salvador before returning to the U.S. with his mother. This early life experience provided Martinez with a unique perspective and shaped his character.

In 2002, Martinez made a pivotal decision to join the U.S. Army, where he served as a Military Occupation Specialist in the 502nd Infantry Regiment of the 101st Airborne Division. His time in the military was marked by dedication and a strong sense of duty.

Tragically, in April 2003, Martinez’s life took a dramatic turn when he sustained severe burns and smoke inhalation from a roadside bomb while serving in Iraq. The injuries were life-altering and required extensive medical treatment and surgeries.

“Every day is a new day. I had to learn how to walk again, how to talk again. When you go through those experiences, you learn to value life in a completely different way.”

Martinez’s journey to recovery led him to the renowned Institute of Surgical Research Burn Center at Brooke Army Medical Center in San Antonio, Texas. Despite the challenges he faced, Martinez’s resilience and strength allowed him to overcome his injuries and inspire others through his remarkable story of survival and perseverance. His early life experiences and military service have played a significant role in shaping the person he is today.
