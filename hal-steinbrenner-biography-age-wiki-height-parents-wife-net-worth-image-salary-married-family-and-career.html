Hal Steinbrenner Biography-Wiki

Hal Steinbrenner is an American businessman who was born on December 3, 1969, in Culver, Indiana, the United States as Harold Zieg Steinbrenner. Steinbrenner is the principal owner, managing general partner, and co-chairman of the New York Yankees of Major League Baseball. Hal and his brother, Hank, inherited the team from their father, George Steinbrenner, who died in 2010.

Hal Steinbrenner Parents -Siblings And Family

Hal is the younger son of George Steinbrenner(Father) and Elizabeth Joan Steinbrenner (Mother). Hal has two sisters, Jessica Steinbrenner and Jennifer Steinbrenner-Swindal.

George Steinbrenner (July 4, 1930 – July 13, 2010) was an American businessman who was the principal owner as well as managing partner of Major League Baseball’s New York Yankees.

On July 13, 2010, the morning of the 2010 Major League Baseball All-Star Game, George Steinbrenner died of a heart attack at St. Joseph’s Hospital in Tampa, Florida.

Joan Steinbrenner (August 25, 1935 – December 14, 2018) was an American philanthropist as well as vice-chair of the New York Yankees baseball team. On May 12, 1956, she married George Steinbrenner, a businessman, and together they had four children. George died in 2010 at the age of 80 years old, while Joan Steinbrenner died on December 14, 2018, at her home in Tampa, Florida.

Steinbrenner Career

Starting in the year 2007, George Steinbrenner gradually ceded day-to-day control of the Yankees to Hal along with his other son, Hank, as his health worsened. Hal was elected Chairman of the Board of Yankee Global Enterprises on September 28, the year 2007.

On November 20, the year 2008, Major League Baseball owners approved the shift of day-to-day control of the Yankees from father George Steinbrenner to Hal.

Hal is Chairman and CEO of Steinbrenner Hotel Properties as well as is on the Board of Directors of the Boys and Girls Club of Tampa Bay. Steinbrenner is a pilot. He owns a GTO single-engine as well as a Cessna high-wing plane.
