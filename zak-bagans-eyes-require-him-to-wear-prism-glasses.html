What Happened To Zak Bagans Eyes?

Zak Bagans eyes have a condition called double vision or diplopia. It is not an inborn condition but something that developed later.

Diplopia is the medical term for double vision or seeing double. It is not a serious condition, but regular follow-up is suggested.

When a person sees two images of a single object, then it may be considered as double vision.

Normally, the eyes, the muscles moving them, the nerves that transport pictures to the brain, and the brain all have to work simultaneously. Any difficulty with any of these parts can result in double vision.

Such a condition can affect one eye (monocular)or both eyes (binocular). It can be horizontal (images appearing side by side) or vertical (images appearing above the other).

Monocular is less common and is usually caused by dry eyes, astigmatism, and cataracts. On the other hand, binoculars are generally a symptom of squint and are caused by eye muscles or nerve disorders.

Treatments for double-vision

Depending upon the cause of the double vision, Optometrists may suggest one or more following treatments;

  • Barring the vision of one eye using an eye patch, occlusive lens, or prism glasses
  • Injecting botulinum toxin
  • Surgery
  • Treating other medical conditions

Zak Wears Cool Prism Glasses

Zak Bagans wears prism glasses to help with his double vision. They look the same as any regular study glasses or other.

His regular glasses are from Oakley.

Prism glasses are doctor-recommended eyeglasses having special lenses for rectifying double vision. Sometimes recommended for eye strains also.

Normally prescribed lenses rectify visual clarity for errors like myopia, hyperopia, or astigmatism. On the contrary, Prism lenses correct eye adjustment or movement problems and not refractive issues.

How does it work?

A prism fitted to eyeglasses bends light before it proceeds through the eye. The light is then altered to the correct spot on the retina in each eye.

The brain then combines the two images jointly to generate one clear image.

Prism glasses for long-term use can be attached to the lens or lenses of a person’s glass. For a short-term prism correction, the Fresnel prism is recommended.

Such glasses may fix the double vision from eye misalignment when caused by:

  • Eye muscle problem
  • Neurological (brain-related) issues
  • Nerve-related difficulty
