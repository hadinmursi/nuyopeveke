Vogue Williams Biography – Vogue Williams Wiki

Vogue Williams is an Irish model and media personality. Williams is well known for participating in Dancing with the Stars, Stepping Out, and for winning the 2015 series of Bear Grylls, Mission Survive. Williams started her media career on November 11, 2010, when she appeared in an Irish reality TV series Fade Street, loosely analogous to The Hills, which tracks the lives of four Irish girls in Dublin.

On April 15, 2012, Williams started her stint in the twelfth series of Dancing with the Stars in Australia, partnered with Christopher Page. In 2013, together with Brian McFadden, they participated in the ITV celebrity dancing competition Stepping Out. They finished in second place. April 3, 2015, Williams won the ITV reality series Bear Grylls: Mission Survive after beating Kelly Holmes and Mike Tindall. In March 2020, Williams became the new host of Sunday Breakfast on Heart FM.

She attended the all-girls Santa Sabina Dominican College in Dublin, Ireland. She holds a degree in construction design and management from the Robert Gordon University in Aberdeen. While there, she interned for six months in London on building sites. Williams later moved to Dublin for further studies in quantity surveying at the Dublin Institute of Technology.

She is the daughter of Freddie and Sandra. Her father was a car salesman. Williams parents were divorced when she was just 7 years. Her father Freddie later died in 2010. Williams is the youngest of three siblings, after brother Frederick and sister Amber. She also has two half-siblings.
